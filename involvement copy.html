AggieSAT Laboratory

At AggieSAT, we design, develop and launch satellites into space. We start at concept development and go all the way to launch vehicle integration. AggieSAT Lab has worked with big players in the industry including NASA, The Aerospace Corporation, and the Air Force Research Laboratory. Since 2005, AggieSAT Lab has successfully launched two satellites into space. The current mission, AggieSAT6 hosts two payloads into Low Earth Orbit (LEO) aboard the 6U Cubesat. The primary payload includes a Radio Frequency (RF) patch antenna array device that generated Direction of Arrival Vectors. The secondary payload is Teledyne’s LowLET Micro-Dosimeter that measures low energy electrons in LEO. The collected data is then downlinked to a ground station in the Aerospace Corporation’s ground station network. Within the team I am an engineering team for the new AggieSAT7 mission currently in the proposal and concept development phase. The team is looking into a mission for the AggieSAT7 CubeSAT around the areas of Space Debris Removal or aiming for a mission through part of the Artemis mission and analyzing the lunar surface for water ice deposits.
